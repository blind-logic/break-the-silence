OUR PURPOSE

Ragging includes any behavior—verbal, physical, or psychological—that harasses, humiliates, or harms a student. It is a criminal offense in India, with severe legal consequences.

This platform, aims to raise awareness, provide support, and empower individuals to take action against ragging. We offer a safe space to report incidents, access resources, and foster a culture of respect, safety, and zero tolerance for bullying. Together, we can create campuses where every student thrives free from fear.

CONSEQUENCES

1. Psychological Impact:

  • Anxiety & Depression: Victims face fear and sadness.
  • PTSD: Severe trauma can cause flashbacks.
  • Low Self-Esteem: Humiliation damages confidence.

2. Social Effects:

  • Isolation: Victims withdraw and feel alienated.
  • Distrust: Abuse affects relationships.
  • Fear of Reporting: Victims fear retaliation.

3. Physical & Academic Impact:

  • Injuries: Some victims suffer harm.
  • Academic Decline: Stress impacts focus and grades.

4. Long-Term Effects:

  • Mental Health Issues: Trauma can cause lasting problems.
  • Career Impact: Effects may carry over into professional life.

Laws Against Ragging

Anti-Ragging Guidelines (UGC Regulations 2009):

  • Form Anti-Ragging Committees and Squads
  • Display awareness posters across the campus
  • Conduct orientation and sensitization programs for students and staff

Punishments for Ragging:

  • Suspension or expulsion
  • Debarment from exams or placements
  • FIR and police action
  • Fine up to ₹25,000 and/or jail up to 3 years
  • Cancellation of admission
  • IPC sections also applicable

Your Rights as a Student:

  • Right to a safe, respectful, and ragging-free environment
  • Freedom to report anonymously without fear
  • Institutions are legally bound to take action and provide support

Applicable IPC Sections:

  • Section 323 – Causing physical hurt
  • Sections 339–341 – Wrongful restraint or confinement
  • Section 354 / 354A – Assault or sexual harassment of women
  • Section 377 – Unnatural sexual offenses
  • Section 294 – Obscene acts in public
  • Section 509 – Insulting the modesty of a woman
  • Section 506 – Criminal intimidation or threats
  • Section 306 – Abetment of suicide
  • Sections 108 / 120B – Abetment and criminal conspiracy
